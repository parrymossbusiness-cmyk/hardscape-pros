Patio & Walkway Installation in Little Rock, AR

Paver, stamped concrete, and natural stone patios and walkways engineered for central Arkansas soil.

Overview

A patio or walkway is one of the highest-return improvements you can make to a Little Rock home, but only if it's built on a base that can handle our soil. Hardscape Pros installs paver patios, stamped concrete patios, flagstone walkways, and concrete walkways across the Little Rock metro, and every project starts the same way: with excavation deep enough and a compacted base thick enough to outlast central Arkansas's expansive clay and freeze-thaw winters.

Most patio failures we get called to fix weren't caused by bad pavers or bad concrete — they were caused by a base that was too thin, a lack of proper drainage, or grading that let water pool against the house. We build every patio and walkway with a compacted crushed-stone base, geotextile fabric where the soil calls for it, and grading that moves water away from your foundation, not toward it. That's true whether you're adding a simple front walkway or a full paver patio built for entertaining.

This page covers our five patio and walkway services. Paver Patio Installation and Stamped Concrete Patios are our two most-requested projects, each with its own dedicated page covering process, cost factors, and design options in depth. Flagstone Walkways, Concrete Walkway Installation, and Fire Pit & Patio Combo Design are detailed in the sections below. Whichever material or layout fits your home, we'll walk your property, assess drainage and grade, and give you a free, itemized estimate before any work begins.

We serve homeowners throughout Little Rock, North Little Rock, Conway, Benton, Sherwood, and the surrounding communities. Every crew member on your job is a Hardscape Pros employee, not a subcontractor pulled in for the day, which is part of why our patios and walkways are backed by a workmanship guarantee.

What Makes a Flagstone Walkway Last Through an Arkansas Winter?

A flagstone walkway lasts through Arkansas winters when it's set on a properly compacted base rather than laid directly on soil. Flagstone is natural stone — typically Arkansas fieldstone or a similar sedimentary stone — cut or selected for relatively flat, irregular pieces that are fitted together with narrow joints. It's one of the most popular walkway materials we install because no two flagstone paths look alike, and the stone holds up well against foot traffic, mowers, and de-icing salt.

The failure point for most flagstone walkways isn't the stone itself, it's the base underneath it. We excavate to a consistent depth, install a compacted gravel base, and either set the stone in a sand setting bed for a traditional dry-laid look or on a mortar bed over a concrete footer for a more formal, rigid installation. Dry-laid flagstone with polymeric sand in the joints handles our region's freeze-thaw cycles better than a mortared walkway with no expansion allowance, so we'll recommend the approach that fits your yard's drainage and how the walkway will be used.

Flagstone walkways typically run from a simple garden path a couple of feet wide to a wider, formal entry walk that ties into a paver or stamped concrete patio. We can match flagstone color and shape to existing landscaping or hardscape elsewhere on the property, and we always grade the base to shed water away from the walkway edges so joints don't erode over time.

How Much Does a Concrete Walkway Cost in Little Rock?

A standard poured concrete walkway in the Little Rock area typically costs less per square foot than pavers or flagstone, which makes it a practical choice for long side-yard paths, utility access walks, or budget-conscious front entries. Cost depends primarily on length, width, thickness, and whether you add a broom finish, exposed aggregate, or a simple decorative edge.

We pour every concrete walkway over a compacted sub-base with control joints spaced to manage cracking as the concrete cures and the ground shifts seasonally. Central Arkansas clay expands and contracts more than sandier soils, so we grade and compact the sub-base carefully before forming and pouring — skipping that step is the most common reason a concrete walkway cracks or settles unevenly within the first few years.

Concrete walkways pair well with a paver or stamped concrete patio as a transition path, or they work as a standalone project connecting a driveway to a front door or a side gate to a backyard. We finish every walkway with a broom or light texture finish for slip resistance, and we can tint or stamp the surface if you want it to coordinate with an existing patio.

Why Combine a Fire Pit With Your Patio Design?

Combining a fire pit with your patio design turns a flat outdoor surface into a destination people actually use after dark and into the cooler months, which is most of the year in central Arkansas. Building the fire pit and patio together, rather than adding a fire pit later, lets us plan seating orientation, paver or concrete patterns, and clearance distances from the start — the fire pit sits on its own footer and non-combustible base, tied visually and structurally into the surrounding hardscape.

We build both wood-burning and gas fire pit options, sized to the patio and the number of people it needs to seat. A typical combo design includes a paver or stamped concrete patio surface, a stone or block fire pit ring with a compacted gravel or paver base rated for heat, and enough clear patio space around the pit for furniture without crowding the flame.

This is one of our most-requested add-ons because it's a relatively small increase in project cost for a large increase in how often the patio gets used. If you're planning a new patio and want a fire pit included, mention it during your free on-site estimate so we can design the layout around it from day one rather than retrofitting later.

FAQ

Patios & Walkways Questions

Can you match a walkway to an existing patio?+

Yes. When we install a walkway that connects to an existing paver or stamped concrete patio, we bring a sample of your current material or finish to match color, pattern, and joint size as closely as possible so the transition looks intentional rather than patched together.

Do paver patios need to be sealed?+

Sealing is optional but recommended. A quality sealer helps prevent stain absorption, reduces polymeric sand washout, and keeps paver color from fading as quickly in direct sun. We offer paver sealing as a standalone service, covered in more detail on our Driveways & Restoration page.

What's the difference between flagstone and stamped concrete?+

Flagstone is natural stone with irregular shapes and real texture variation, while stamped concrete is poured concrete stamped with a mold to mimic stone, brick, or slate. Flagstone tends to cost more but offers a one-of-a-kind natural look; stamped concrete is more budget-friendly and gives you a consistent, seamless surface.
