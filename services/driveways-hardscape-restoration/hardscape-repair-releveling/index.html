Hardscape Repair & Releveling in Little Rock, AR

Fixing sunken, heaved, or shifted pavers and concrete by correcting the base underneath.

Overview

Hardscape repair and releveling is the process of correcting sunken, heaved, or uneven pavers, patios, walkways, and driveways by fixing the base underneath, not just patching the visible surface. Most hardscape damage in central Arkansas traces back to a base that was too thin, poorly compacted, or lacking proper drainage from the original installation, which surface-level patching can't actually resolve.

You likely need repair and releveling if you're seeing individual pavers that have sunk or risen relative to their neighbors, a section of patio or walkway that's become a trip hazard, standing water pooling on a surface that should drain, or a driveway with sections settling unevenly. These issues typically get worse over time as water continues to erode or saturate the compromised base area.

Our repair process starts by removing the affected pavers or cutting out the damaged concrete section, then excavating down to assess why the original base failed — often inadequate depth, poor compaction, or a drainage issue that's been saturating the base repeatedly. We correct the base with properly compacted aggregate, add drainage where water infiltration was the root cause, and then reset the original pavers or pour new concrete to match the surrounding surface.

Because we're addressing the underlying cause rather than just the symptom, a proper hardscape repair should hold up as well as new construction in that section. We'll assess whether your project needs a targeted repair or whether the base failure is widespread enough that full replacement makes more financial sense, and we'll give you an honest recommendation either way during your free on-site estimate.

Repair cost depends mainly on how much surface area is affected and how deep the base correction needs to go, not just the visible size of the sunken or heaved area. A small isolated dip might only need a few pavers lifted and reset, while a repair tied to a drainage failure may require excavating a wider area to fix the source of the water. We'll explain exactly what we find once we open up the affected section.

FAQ

Hardscape Repair & Releveling Questions

Is it cheaper to repair or replace a failing patio or driveway?+

Repair is almost always less expensive than full replacement when the damage is isolated to one or a few sections. We'll assess the extent of base failure during your estimate and recommend whichever option makes more financial sense for your situation.

Why did my patio or driveway settle in the first place?+

The most common causes are an original base that was too thin or poorly compacted, or a drainage issue that repeatedly saturated the base beneath the surface. We diagnose the specific cause before repairing so the fix actually holds.
