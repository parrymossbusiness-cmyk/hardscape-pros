Paver Driveway Installation in Little Rock, AR

Interlocking pavers on a vehicle-rated compacted base, built to handle daily traffic without shifting.

Overview

A paver driveway is built from interlocking concrete or clay brick units set over a compacted aggregate base engineered specifically to handle vehicle loads, which is a thicker and more heavily compacted base than what's used under a paver patio. Because the surface is made of individual units, a paver driveway can be repaired section by section if a specific area is damaged, rather than requiring a full slab replacement.

Homeowners choose a paver driveway when replacing a cracked or aging asphalt or concrete driveway, when building a new driveway and wanting a more distinctive look than plain concrete, or when they want a surface that can be repaired in sections rather than as a whole. Pavers also handle utility cuts well, since a section can be lifted, the utility work completed, and the same pavers reset.

We excavate driveways to a greater depth than patios to accommodate a thicker compacted aggregate base rated for vehicle loads, typically compacting the base in multiple lifts and checking density at each stage rather than compacting once at full depth. Edge restraints are installed along the full perimeter to prevent the outer pavers from spreading under vehicle weight, and we grade the finished surface for positive drainage toward the street or a designated drain rather than toward your garage.

Paver selection matters for a driveway more than for a patio, since not every paver product is rated for vehicle traffic — we use pavers manufactured and rated for driveway use, in a thickness appropriate for standard vehicle loads. We'll go over paver style, pattern, and border options, along with realistic maintenance expectations, during your free on-site estimate.

Driveway size, base depth required for your specific soil, and any drainage correction needed are the main factors driving project cost and timeline. A straightforward single-car driveway on stable soil moves faster than a wider driveway that needs significant regrading, and we'll spell out both the cost and the schedule clearly in your written estimate before work begins.

FAQ

Paver Driveway Installation Questions

Are paver driveways strong enough for daily vehicle use?+

Yes, when built with a properly compacted, vehicle-rated base and pavers manufactured for driveway use. The base depth and compaction, not just the paver itself, are what determine whether a driveway holds up to daily traffic.

How do you repair a paver driveway if one section settles?+

We lift the affected pavers, correct and recompact the base underneath, then reset the same pavers so the repair blends into the surrounding driveway rather than requiring a full replacement.
