Outdoor Kitchen Construction in Little Rock, AR

Built-in grill islands with stone veneer, storage, and counter space designed around how you cook outdoors.

Overview

An outdoor kitchen is a built-in cooking and food-prep structure, typically a masonry or block-framed island finished in stone, brick, or stucco veneer, housing a built-in grill along with counter space, storage, and often a sink or additional appliances. It's a step beyond a standalone grill because it's a permanent structure tied into your patio, with utilities planned in from the start.

Homeowners usually add an outdoor kitchen when they're already grilling and entertaining outside often enough that hauling a portable grill and folding table out each time has become the bottleneck. It's also a natural addition when building a new patio, since planning the kitchen and patio together avoids retrofitting gas or electrical lines into a finished surface later.

We build every outdoor kitchen on its own poured concrete footing sized for the structure's weight, framed in concrete block, and finished in a stone, brick, or stucco veneer that matches your home or patio. Utility planning happens early — we coordinate gas line runs for the grill or a side burner, electrical for lighting and small appliances, and plumbing if a sink is included, all before the block framing goes up so nothing has to be cut into finished masonry later.

Counter material, storage configuration, and appliance selection are all decisions we walk through with you before construction starts, since retrofitting an outdoor kitchen after the fact is far more disruptive than planning it up front. Central Arkansas's humidity and freeze-thaw winters mean we pay close attention to sealing joints and using freeze-rated materials so the structure holds up through every season, not just summer grilling weather.

Most outdoor kitchen islands run six to twelve feet long depending on the appliances included, and we size the footing and framing to that layout before ordering materials. If you're planning to add a pergola or fireplace nearby, mention it during your free on-site estimate so we can plan sightlines, shade, and smoke direction as one connected space rather than three separate additions.

FAQ

Outdoor Kitchen Construction Questions

Do I need a permit for an outdoor kitchen?+

Most outdoor kitchens with gas or electrical lines require a permit in the Little Rock metro. We handle the permitting process as part of the project and confirm requirements for your address during the estimate.

Can an outdoor kitchen be added to an existing patio?+

Yes, as long as the patio's base and layout can accommodate the kitchen's footing and utility runs. We'll assess your existing patio during the free on-site estimate to confirm what's possible.
