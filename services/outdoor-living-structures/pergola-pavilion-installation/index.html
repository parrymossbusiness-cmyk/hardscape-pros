Pergola & Pavilion Installation in Little Rock, AR

Cedar, cellular PVC, or metal-frame pergolas and pavilions set on footings built to last.

Overview

A pergola is an open-roof structure of vertical posts supporting horizontal cross-beams that provide partial shade and a defined outdoor space, while a pavilion is a similar freestanding structure with a solid roof for full weather coverage. Both extend how much of the year you can comfortably use a patio, by cutting direct sun in summer or providing shelter from light rain.

Homeowners typically add a pergola when a patio gets too much direct afternoon sun to sit outside comfortably, or when they want a defined structure to anchor an outdoor living space without fully enclosing it. A pavilion is the right call when you want more complete weather protection, often for a space that includes an outdoor kitchen or a larger seating and dining area.

We build both in cedar for a natural wood look, cellular PVC for a low-maintenance painted finish, or powder-coated metal framing for a modern, minimal profile. Every structure sits on its own poured concrete footings, sized and set below frost depth so seasonal ground movement doesn't shift or rack the posts over time. For house-attached pergolas, we flash the attachment ledger board properly to prevent water intrusion into your home's structure, a detail that's frequently done wrong on lower-quality installations.

Beam spacing and post placement are planned around both structural load and the amount of shade you want — tighter beam spacing blocks more sun, while wider spacing lets more light through for plants or a softer shade pattern. We'll go over material, size, and attachment options during your free on-site estimate, including whether a retractable canopy or polycarbonate roof panel addition makes sense for your climate needs.

Size and material are the biggest cost factors on a pergola or pavilion project. A standard 12-by-14-foot cedar pergola is a common starting point for a patio seating area, while larger pavilions built to cover an outdoor kitchen or dining set require more substantial framing and footings. We'll measure your space and walk through material tradeoffs before putting together your written estimate.

FAQ

Pergola & Pavilion Installation Questions

What's the difference between a pergola and a pavilion?+

A pergola has an open, slatted roof that provides partial shade, while a pavilion has a solid roof for full weather protection. Pergolas suit homeowners who want definition and partial shade; pavilions suit those who want a fully covered outdoor room.

Can a pergola be attached to my house?+

Yes, we build both freestanding and house-attached pergolas. Attached structures require a properly flashed ledger board connection, which we install to prevent water intrusion into your home.
